# The Manifesto Series Prompt Pack

Use this prompt pack to generate Reflection Friday manifesto ideas and full post drafts for Coach Kagiso.

## Best Workflow

Do not ask the AI to create the whole year in one response. It will become generic.

Use this workflow instead:

1. Run the Monthly Idea Bank Prompt for one month.
2. Review the ideas and choose the strongest 4 or 5.
3. Run the Full Post Expansion Prompt for each selected idea.
4. Save the final headline, handwritten note, LinkedIn post, and visual direction.

This gives you control while still letting the AI generate strong options.

---

## Brand Context For Every Prompt

Copy this into every manifesto prompt.

```text
You are helping create content for Kagiso Shabangu, a South African Career Development and Personal Brand Coach.

Kagiso helps professionals who feel stuck, under-positioned, unseen, unsupported, or unsure of their next career move. Her audience includes early-career, mid-career, and emerging leadership professionals in South Africa, especially people who want career clarity, stronger positioning, better visibility, leadership confidence, mentorship, and practical next steps.

The content series is called:
THE MANIFESTO SERIES: SEASON ONE

The weekly ritual is:
Reflection Friday

The format:
Every Friday, Kagiso publishes one reflective manifesto-style post. The post should feel like a quiet but powerful note to professionals who are trying to move with more clarity and intention.

The visual concept:
- Small label: notes | reflection friday
- Series label: the manifesto series
- Monthly title: e.g. The Career Clarity Manifesto
- Big graphic headline: one strong belief statement
- Handwritten note: one short intimate note, as if written on a torn piece of paper
- Main platform content: a LinkedIn-ready text post

Kagiso's voice:
- Warm, direct, reflective, practical, and professional.
- South African professional reality must be felt without using slang heavily.
- She does not sound like a corporate influencer.
- She does not use generic motivation.
- She does not use hustle language.
- She does not use corporate jargon.
- She does not over-explain.
- She teaches through honest reflection.
- She can be vulnerable without being dramatic.

Use these voice patterns:
- "Your career matters."
- "It's possible."
- "Reflect. Research. Reach out."
- "No one truly makes it alone."
- "Sometimes we do not need big changes. We need small steps, better information, and the courage to try again."
- Close the LinkedIn post with: "That is my reflection for Friday."

Avoid:
- em dashes
- cheap motivation
- wellness language like abundance, manifestation, high vibration
- phrases like hustle, grind, secure the bag
- generic LinkedIn bro formatting
- too many emojis
- fake case studies
- invented statistics
- over-selling services

Core content pillars:
1. Career Growth and Strategy: career clarity, career pivots, job search, promotion readiness, getting unstuck.
2. Leadership and People Development: leadership presence, influence, confidence, managing up, new managers, feedback, leading people.
3. Personal Brand and Visibility: LinkedIn, CV, professional reputation, positioning, being seen for the right opportunities.
4. Mentorship and Community: support systems, networks, guidance, community, not building alone.
```

## Prompt 1: Monthly Idea Bank

Use this when you want many ideas for a specific month before choosing what to write.

```text
Use the brand context below and create a complete idea bank for the selected month of The Manifesto Series.

[PASTE BRAND CONTEXT HERE]

MONTH BRIEF:
[PASTE ONE MONTH BRIEF FROM THE MONTHLY BLOCKS BELOW]

TASK:
Create a monthly idea bank for this manifesto month.

Output the following:

1. Monthly Strategy
- One paragraph explaining what this month is emotionally and strategically about.
- What the audience should start believing by the end of the month.
- Which of Kagiso's four pillars this month supports.

2. 12 Manifesto Post Ideas
For each idea, give:
- Idea number
- Friday fit: early month, middle month, late month, or closing post
- Post angle
- Audience pain point
- Core belief shift
- Graphic headline, max 14 words
- Handwritten note, max 22 words
- LinkedIn hook, 1 to 2 lines
- Short summary of the post
- Best visual direction
- Why this idea is worth saving

3. Recommended Friday Calendar
Choose the strongest ideas for the exact Fridays in this month.
For each Friday, give:
- Date
- Selected idea number
- Manifesto post title
- Graphic headline
- Handwritten note
- One sentence explaining why this should be posted on that Friday

4. Backup Ideas
Give 3 backup ideas that can be used if the month needs extra posts.

5. Content Quality Check
Score the recommended posts from 1 to 10 on:
- Emotional resonance
- Career relevance
- Save/share potential
- Alignment with Coach Kagiso's voice
- Ability to become part of the year-end free guide

Rules:
- Do not write full LinkedIn posts yet unless I ask.
- Keep the ideas specific, not broad.
- Do not repeat the same angle in different words.
- Make the headlines strong enough to work on a square graphic.
- Make the handwritten notes intimate, but not cheesy.
- Make the ideas feel collectible as part of a future free guide.
```

Manifesto title:
The Career Clarity Manifesto

Graphic headline:

Handwritten note:
Love, your next move does not need to be loud. It needs to be honest.

LinkedIn post:
Sometimes the hardest part is admitting that the version of success you were chasing no longer fits.

Not because you failed.
Not because you are ungrateful.
Not because you are confused beyond help.

Sometimes you have simply grown, and your goals have not caught up with who you are becoming.

I think many professionals stay stuck because they are waiting for clarity to arrive fully formed. But clarity often starts smaller than that.

It starts with one honest sentence:
"This no longer feels aligned."

Then another:
"I need to understand what I want next."

Then one small action:
Reflect. Research. Reach out.

If this week has been showing you that something needs to shift, do not rush past that feeling. Sit with it. It may not be the full answer, but it might be the beginning of a better question.

That is my reflection for Friday.
