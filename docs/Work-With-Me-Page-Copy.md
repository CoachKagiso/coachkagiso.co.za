## WHY THIS COPY WORKS — design and copy notes for the AI builder

These notes are not for the page. They're for whoever builds the page so they understand the choices.

**Voice consistency:** Copy uses Kagiso's actual phrases — *"your career matters" energy in the audit signpost, "show up" in the headline, "intentional" feel throughout. No "synergy," "leverage," or generic motivation language.

**Em dash count across all copy:** zero. Replaced with periods, commas, or restructured sentences.

**No rule-of-three patterns** built for rhythm only. Where lists of three appear (e.g. "3 specific fixes / 3 strengths"), they describe actual product structure, not decorative cadence.

**Pricing visible up front:** every product shows the price in the heading. No "click for pricing" or hidden tiers.

**Clear differentiation between the two R150 products:** CV Review is feedback, Cover Letter is a written deliverable. The taglines do the work of separating them so buyers don't get confused.

**The Bundle visually highlighted** as the recommended option for active job seekers. Standard pricing-page convention. Worth styling slightly differently (subtle border in Rodeo Dust, or a small "Most popular for job seekers" tag) so the eye lands on it first.

**Tracks not tiers:** the page organises by job-to-be-done, not by price. Buyers self-select by problem, then choose depth within their track. This matches how people actually buy career coaching.

**Career Clarity gets its own track** even though it's a single product. That visual breathing room signals it's a different category from the document services. Important for repositioning Kagiso away from "CV lady" framing.

**Saturday Masterclass kept on this page** rather than its own page (per the multi-page architecture decision: /work-with-me is the parent, /masterclass anchors to the masterclass section for ad deep-linking but the same copy lives on the parent).

**The accessibility footer note** addresses the affordability question without permanent discounting. Mentions the R150 review, payment plans, and the scholarship model in one paragraph. Keeps it warm without making it the headline.

**No CTAs labelled "Submit" or "Learn More."** Every CTA names the action the buyer is taking ("Book my Clarity Session," "Get the Bundle," "Hold my spot"). Per the copywriting skill rules.

**The "Not sure which one fits?" question at the bottom** routes undecided visitors to the audit instead of forcing a buying decision. Lower-pressure conversion path. The audit then surfaces their archetype and points them back to the right package.
