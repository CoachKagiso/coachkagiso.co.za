# Lead Magnet Plan — Kagiso Shabangu

**A strategic plan for the free downloadable that captures emails, demonstrates her thinking, and qualifies leads for her paid services.**

Version 1.0 · April 2026 · Companion to the Instructor Playbook v1.3 and Content Strategy v1.0

---

## What a lead magnet is actually for

A lead magnet has four jobs, in this priority order:

1. **Capture an email.** Without that, none of the other jobs matter.
2. **Demonstrate her thinking.** The reader needs to walk away saying "this person knows what they're doing." If they don't, the email is useless because they won't open future emails.
3. **Pre-qualify the lead.** Surface what kind of buyer they are — stuck graduate, mid-career stalled, career changer — so future emails can be relevant.
4. **Create a clear next step.** Not generic ("book a call"). Specific to where they are after consuming the magnet.

A lead magnet does **NOT** solve the problem. If it solves the problem, the buyer doesn't need her paid services. The magnet should *diagnose the problem clearly* and *start the work*, leaving the deeper transformation to the masterclass and 1-on-1 packages.

This is the most common lead-magnet mistake: solo coaches give away their best framework, the reader feels satisfied, the email goes unread for six months, and the buyer never converts. The fix is to design the magnet around *clarity* rather than *resolution*.

## What's already crowded in the SA market

Quick scan of what every other SA career coach is already giving away for free:

- "10 SA CV templates"
- "Top 50 interview questions"
- "5 mistakes that get your CV rejected"
- Generic LinkedIn checklists
- "How to write a cover letter" PDFs

All of this is Googleable. None of it differentiates anyone. If Kagiso ships a "10 CV mistakes" PDF, she joins the noise.

**What's missing from the SA market:**

- **Diagnostic tools.** Self-assessments that help the reader figure out *what's actually going on with them* before they decide what to fix.
- **Real before-and-afters.** Most coaches don't show their work — proprietary protectionism. A real example with annotations is rare and valued.
- **Frameworks for non-CV career problems.** Career stagnation, mid-career pivots, the "stuck but employed" problem.
- **SA-specific opinionated curation.** Not "here are 50 free courses" but "here are the only 7 worth your weekend, and here's what to skip."
- **Tools that help people decide, not just do.** Most career content is about action. Less is about clarity.

The strongest opportunity is in **diagnostic tools** — they're under-served in SA, and they're naturally proprietary because the framework itself is the value.

## Lead magnet criteria checklist

Anything we ship as a lead magnet must pass these tests:

- [ ] **Original framework.** Built on her thinking, not Googleable.
- [ ] **SA-specific.** Examples, references, terminology that locate it in her market.
- [ ] **Quick to consume.** Under 30 minutes start to finish, ideally 10–15.
- [ ] **Surfaces a clear archetype.** The reader should know *which kind of stuck they are* by the end.
- [ ] **Has a specific next step.** Tied to a paid product, by name, with a link.
- [ ] **Reusable across cohorts.** Built once, valid for at least 12 months without major updates.
- [ ] **Visually branded.** Matches the new palette (Dark Gunmetal, Rodeo Dust, Chai) and typography (Noto Serif Display + Montserrat).
- [ ] **Non-controversial.** No claims about her employer, no industry-specific framing, no fake testimonials.

## Four candidate concepts (ranked)

### Option 1 — The 5-Minute Career Diagnostic ★ RECOMMENDED

**Pillar:** Career Growth & Strategy (primary), Personal Branding (secondary)

**Format:** 12–15 page interactive PDF (self-scoring, decision-flow style). Designed to feel like a real-world assessment even though it's a static document.

**Value to the reader:** They take a 10-question diagnostic, score themselves, and discover which of 5 career archetypes they fit. Each archetype comes with a tailored 7-day action and a clear "if this is you, here's what to do next."

**Why it works:**
- Diagnostic format is rare in the SA coaching market — high differentiation
- The output is *personalised* because the reader's score routes them to a specific archetype, even though the document is static
- Pre-qualifies the lead beautifully — Kagiso knows what kind of buyer they are before any sales conversation
- Each archetype maps cleanly to a paid product (Clarity for one, VIP for another, Masterclass for a third) so the upsell path is obvious
- Self-scoring is high-engagement — readers actively interact, which makes them remember her brand

**Why it's hard to Google:** The diagnostic itself is original. The five archetypes are her framework. The recommended actions are her thinking. None of this exists online for SA professionals.

**Effort to build:** 25–35 hours total — 8 hours for content design (questions, scoring, archetypes, actions), 12 hours for Canva layout, 5 hours for review and revision. Designed once, reused for years.

**Risk:** None significant.

**Recommendation:** Build this one.

---

### Option 2 — The South African Career Reset Workbook

**Pillar:** Career Growth & Strategy

**Format:** 25–35 page workbook PDF. Walks the reader through a complete career re-evaluation in five sections.

**Value to the reader:** A structured, written exercise that takes them from "I'm stuck" to a written 30-day plan.

**Why it works:**
- Comprehensive — feels like real value
- Multi-section structure means reader spends 60+ minutes inside her brand
- Workbook format is high-engagement
- Can include scripts, templates, and frameworks

**Why it's risky:**
- *Too comprehensive.* This solves a meaningful chunk of her audience's problem. After completing it, some readers won't need her paid services.
- Production-heavy. 30+ pages of original content + design = 50–80 hours of work for V1.
- Feels closer to a paid product than a lead magnet — actually a strong candidate for the **R249 self-paced PDF in Q4 2026** (per Part 16 of the playbook).

**Recommendation:** Don't build as the lead magnet. Save this concept for the Q4 2026 paid product.

---

### Option 3 — Real CV Before-and-Afters with Annotations

**Pillar:** Personal Branding & Visibility

**Format:** 15–20 page PDF showing 3–5 fully-anonymised CVs she's revamped, with side-by-side before/after pages and her annotated reasoning.

**Value to the reader:** Sees her actual thinking applied to real work. Demonstrates expertise more powerfully than any framework can.

**Why it works:**
- Before-and-afters convert. People love to see transformation.
- Hard to fake. Builds genuine credibility.
- Naturally leads to her CV Revamp service (R400) and LinkedIn Package (R500).

**Why it's risky:**
- **Permission and anonymisation are non-trivial.** Even fully anonymised CVs need explicit written permission from the original owner. Otherwise legal exposure.
- **Reinforces the "she's just a CV person" framing** we're actively moving away from. The brand is broader (leadership, mentorship, training, MCing). A CV-focused lead magnet pulls her positioning back to CV land.
- Production effort is moderate (20–25 hours).

**Recommendation:** Don't build now. Reconsider in 12 months once the broader brand is established and CV is one of several services, not the headline. At that point, this becomes a strong second lead magnet specifically targeting the CV/LinkedIn segment of her audience.

---

### Option 4 — The Personal Brand Audit

**Pillar:** Personal Branding & Visibility

**Format:** 10-page checklist + scoring tool. Reader audits their own LinkedIn and CV against 30 criteria, scores themselves, and gets a "personal brand grade."

**Value to the reader:** Concrete, immediately actionable. Tells them exactly where their visible profile is weak.

**Why it works:**
- Quick (under 15 minutes)
- Self-scoring builds engagement
- Naturally feeds into LinkedIn Package and CV Revamp services
- Can include before/after examples without needing client permission

**Why it's weaker than Option 1:**
- Narrower in scope — only addresses the personal branding pillar
- Lower differentiation — "audit your LinkedIn" lead magnets exist already in SA market
- Less qualifying value for higher-tier products (Clarity, VIP) which aren't primarily about personal brand

**Recommendation:** Strong as a *second* lead magnet later, specifically for the Personal Branding pillar. Not as good as Option 1 for the first one.

## The recommended lead magnet — designed in detail

### Title

**"The 5-Minute Career Diagnostic — Are You Stuck, Stalling, or About to Pivot?"**

The title needs to do three things at once:
- Promise speed (5 minutes — low cognitive cost)
- Promise clarity (diagnostic — answer at the end)
- Use her audience's actual language ("stuck," "stalling," "pivot" — these are her words, repeatedly used in her TikToks and posts)

Alternative title (test against the first):
**"Stuck, Stalling, or Pivoting? Find Out in 5 Minutes."**

Slightly punchier. Same content. Same promise. Pick whichever feels more on-brand to her after she sees both.

### Length and format

**12–15 pages, PDF, A4 portrait. Designed in Canva.**

Reader completion time: 7–12 minutes (with the "5-minute" framing being a slight under-promise — under-promising and over-delivering builds trust).

### Structure

**Page 1 — Cover**
- Title in Noto Serif Display 700, Dark Gunmetal
- Subtitle: "A self-administered diagnostic for South Africans who feel stuck in their careers"
- A short by-line: "Built by Kagiso Shabangu, Career Development & Personal Brand Coach"
- The tagline at the bottom: "Show up. Stand out. Level up."

**Page 2 — The Premise**
- One paragraph that names the problem: "Most career advice tells you what to do. Almost none of it helps you figure out what's actually going on with you. This diagnostic doesn't tell you what to do. It tells you which kind of stuck you are — because the answer is different for each one."
- A reassurance: "You'll need 5 minutes, a pen, and honesty. Nothing more."

**Page 3 — How to Use This**
- Instructions: read each question, choose A/B/C/D, write your letter down. At the end, score yourself.
- A clear visual showing the structure of the document.

**Pages 4–8 — The 10 Questions**
- One or two questions per page, with generous whitespace
- Each question gets four possible answers (A/B/C/D)
- Questions are designed to surface different career archetypes — not test knowledge, but reveal patterns

**Sample questions** (these are starting drafts — Kagiso refines based on her own coaching experience):

1. When you imagine your career a year from now, what comes up first?
   - A) The same role, slightly better at it
   - B) A different role at the same company
   - C) A different industry entirely
   - D) Honestly? I have no idea.

2. The last time you learned something new at work was…
   - A) This week
   - B) This month
   - C) Earlier this year
   - D) I genuinely can't remember.

3. When a recruiter messages you on LinkedIn, you usually…
   - A) Reply, even if you're not looking
   - B) Read the message and ignore it
   - C) Don't see it for weeks
   - D) Don't get LinkedIn messages.

4. The thing that frustrates you most about your career right now is…
   - A) I'm not growing fast enough
   - B) My value isn't being recognised
   - C) The whole industry feels wrong
   - D) I don't even know what I want

5. If you got a R5,000 raise tomorrow, would the feeling that something is off go away?
   - A) Mostly, yes
   - B) For a few months, then no
   - C) No — money isn't the issue
   - D) I'd take it but I still wouldn't be happy

6. The last time you reached out to someone outside your immediate work circle for career advice was…
   - A) This month
   - B) This year
   - C) Years ago
   - D) Never

7. Your CV was last updated…
   - A) In the last 3 months
   - B) Earlier this year
   - C) Over a year ago
   - D) I don't even know where it is

8. When you describe what you do at work to a friend, you…
   - A) Have a clean elevator pitch ready
   - B) Roughly explain it
   - C) Struggle to explain why anyone would care
   - D) Avoid the topic

9. The thought of staying in your current role for another two years feels…
   - A) Reasonable
   - B) Tolerable
   - C) Suffocating
   - D) Devastating

10. If a mentor offered you 30 minutes of their time tomorrow, would you have a specific question ready?
    - A) Yes, exactly one
    - B) Yes, but a vague one
    - C) I'd waste the time
    - D) I wouldn't take the meeting

**Page 9 — Scoring**
- A simple table that tells the reader to count their A/B/C/D answers
- The dominant letter (most answers) determines their archetype
- If it's a tie, instructions on which to favour

**Pages 10–12 — The Five Archetypes**

Each archetype gets one full page with:
- A name and a short tagline
- A "this is you when…" description (3–4 sentences that name the experience precisely)
- A "what's actually going on" diagnosis (2–3 sentences)
- A "your one next step this week" — specific, actionable, free
- A "what would help you most" — referencing one of her paid products by name

The five archetypes:

**1. The Plateaued Performer** (mostly A's)
*"You're doing well at what you do. You've stopped getting better at it. You don't need a new job — you need a new chapter inside the one you have."*
- Diagnosis: Career stagnation in plain sight. The job isn't broken; the trajectory is.
- Next step this week: Identify one stretch project you could volunteer for in the next 30 days.
- What would help most: **The Saturday Masterclass — From Stuck to Strategic** (R450 early bird).

**2. The Quiet Pivoter** (mostly B's)
*"You're not unhappy. You're not energised either. You sense your value isn't being seen — and you're starting to wonder if it's the company, the manager, or you."*
- Diagnosis: Visibility problem, not a performance problem.
- Next step this week: Audit your LinkedIn headline against what you actually do.
- What would help most: **LinkedIn Optimisation & CV Revamp Package** (R500).

**3. The Burnt-Out Builder** (mostly C's)
*"You've been carrying things — projects, people, expectations — for a long time. You're tired in a way that a holiday won't fix. You need a different shape of career, not a longer break."*
- Diagnosis: Industry or role mismatch, not exhaustion.
- Next step this week: Write down the three parts of your current role that drain you, and the three that don't.
- What would help most: **Career Clarity Package — 60-min 1-on-1** (R800).

**4. The Lost Pivoter** (mostly D's)
*"You know something is wrong. You don't know what to fix. Every piece of advice feels generic because nobody actually understands your situation."*
- Diagnosis: You don't have a content problem — you have a clarity problem.
- Next step this week: Stop reading career advice for one week. Write three pages by hand about what your day looked like 5 years ago vs. now.
- What would help most: **Glow Up VIP Package — full strategic reset** (R1,200).

**5. The Engaged Strategist** (a mix, leaning A and B)
*"You're not stuck — you're sharpening. You know what you want, you're moving toward it, and you're looking for accountability, not direction."*
- Diagnosis: You're already in motion. You need fellowship, not therapy.
- Next step this week: Tell two people what you're working on this quarter.
- What would help most: **The Saturday Masterclass series + Saturday Crew community** (R450 / session).

**Page 13 — What Now**
- A consolidated list of her four paid products with one-line descriptions
- A clear booking link for each
- Her contact details: hello@coachkagiso.co.za, WhatsApp number, LinkedIn

**Page 14 — A Personal Note**
- Two short paragraphs from Kagiso. Not sales copy. The thing that makes her brand different.
- Sample: *"I built this diagnostic because most career advice I've seen tries to fix you before it understands you. That's the wrong way around. Whichever archetype you scored as, you're not broken — you're just at a particular point on a path. The work is figuring out which path, and what the next 30 days looks like. If you want help with that, I'm here. If you don't, take what's useful from this and run."*
- Signed: Kagiso *(this is the only place where the Northwell brush font appears, per the brand brief)*

**Page 15 — Footer**
- Brand assets: tagline, website, social handles
- A small "Reading this on a phone? Save it to your home screen for later" note

### Design specs

- Cover: Chai background (#E4D8CB), Noto Serif Display 700 title, Rodeo Dust accents
- Inside pages: White background, Dark Gunmetal text, Chai panels for question backgrounds
- Visual elements: simple line-style icons for each archetype, all in Rodeo Dust
- Typography: Noto Serif Display for headings only, Montserrat for everything else, Northwell for the signature on Page 14 only
- Page numbers in Latte at the bottom of every page

### Distribution

**On the website:**
- Footer of every page: a small CTA "Take the 5-Minute Career Diagnostic →"
- Resources/Lead-Magnet page: full landing page with the title, a 3-bullet description, and the email form
- Hero secondary CTA: "Download CV Checklist" should become "Take the Diagnostic"

**On TikTok:**
- One pinned video: 60 seconds explaining the diagnostic, with text on screen "Link in bio."
- Three follow-up videos over launch week: one per archetype, with a teaser of what that archetype reveals.

**On LinkedIn:**
- A long-form post explaining why she built it and the five archetypes
- A carousel with the archetype names and one-line descriptions
- A second post one week later sharing patterns from early downloaders ("Most of you scored as Quiet Pivoters — here's what that means")

**On Facebook:**
- One repost of the LinkedIn long-form, rewritten as a personal story

**Via Brevo (email automation):**
- Day 0 (download): The PDF + a thank-you email + a follow-up question ("What did you score as?")
- Day 3: Story email — "Here's the most common archetype, and what it usually means"
- Day 7: Direct CTA — "If you scored [archetype], here's the offer for you"
- Day 14: Bridge to the Saturday Masterclass / next product

### Measurement — what success looks like

| Metric | Month 1 | Month 3 | Month 6 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Downloads | 50 | 200 | 500 |
| Email-list size | 50 | 200 | 500 |
| Conversion to a paid product | 5% | 8% | 10% |
| Replies to the Day-3 follow-up | 10% | 15% | 20% |

If downloads stall below 50 in month one, the marketing rollout needs more push, not the magnet. If downloads are healthy but conversions are below 5%, the archetype-to-product mapping isn't working — refine the next-step CTAs.

## Why we're not just doing a CV checklist

Quick honest note in case anyone proposes "let's just ship a CV checklist instead — it's faster":

A CV checklist:
- Is Googleable (every other coach has one)
- Reinforces the "she's a CV person" framing we're moving away from
- Doesn't qualify the lead beyond "someone who wants a better CV"
- Doesn't naturally lead to her higher-priced products (Clarity at R800, VIP at R1,200)
- Builds an email list of people who think they have a CV problem, when most of them actually have a clarity problem

The diagnostic does the opposite of all four. It costs roughly the same to build. Build the diagnostic.
