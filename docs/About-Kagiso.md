
## About Kagiso

**I'm Kagiso Shabangu. I help people get unstuck.**

### 1. Where I come from

I grew up in Soweto, on the dusty streets of Diepkloof and Zola. I didn't grow up with a clear path. I grew up having to figure out a lot of things myself.

For a long time, that felt like a disadvantage. I didn't have the networks. I didn't know how to knock on doors or have the right conversations. I only started to figure it out in my thirties — how to build real connections, how to be seen for what I could actually do, how to stop waiting for someone to notice me.

That journey taught me something I now believe completely: nobody should have to figure this out alone.

### 2. The moment everything shifted

I was good at leadership. I knew it. People around me told me. But every time I applied for a leadership role, the door closed. Not once. Six times.

I assumed being good at the work would be enough. It wasn't. I was capable, but I didn't know what skills I was missing, or what kind of person I needed to become. I didn't know who to ask.

The seventh time, I did the work differently. I stopped applying blindly. I started talking to the right people. I asked someone already inside the room to tell me honestly what I wasn't seeing. I got the role.

That was my wake-up call. The rules I thought I was playing by — work hard, deliver, wait — weren't the rules at all. I'd spent years being good at my job and completely invisible to the people making decisions.

I promised myself that once I understood how the game actually worked, I'd teach it.

### 3. What I believe

**Hard work alone is not enough.**

You can be the brightest person in the room, shoot the lights out on your KPIs, and still be the person nobody thinks of when a role opens. I've lived that.

**I also don't believe your next opportunity comes from someone else.** It comes from you — from doing the work when no one is watching, from putting in the time, from having the right conversations.

The problem isn't your talent. It's that talent without visibility is just private potential. You need people in the right rooms to know your name. You need to do the foundation work — cleaning up how you present yourself, closing the skills gaps you don't even know you have.

Most career advice stops too early. It tells you to fix your CV. It doesn't ask why you're exhausted, or why you've lost confidence, or whether anyone at your level ever gets promoted in your company. My coaching takes a wider view. Your career doesn't exist in a vacuum.

I have honest conversations. I refuse to let people sit in what I call the lazy chair — waiting for a break that isn't coming. I'll show you where you've been sabotaging yourself, and I'll give you the plan to move differently.

### 4. Who I work with

I started out wanting to help graduates and people who couldn't afford traditional career support. I know what it feels like to need something and not have access to it. I've got a deep heart for unemployed people because I've known that feeling personally.

Before I ever charged for coaching, I mentored over ten people for free. Some were completely stuck. Some had been applying for months without a callback. We worked together until they moved.

Over time, the people who found me weren't just graduates. They were professionals who'd been working for years, earning decent money, and quietly losing momentum. People who knew they were capable of more but couldn't name why nothing was shifting.

That's who I work with now. The stuck. The overlooked. The ones who've been doing good work that nobody seems to notice.

### 5. How I work

Every client starts with a question. I ask you to imagine your face on a freeway billboard — thousands of people driving past it every day. What do you want them to think? Who is that person?

Most people don't have the answer immediately. That's fine. The question lingers.

From there, I take a holistic view. I look at your CV, your LinkedIn, your skills — and also your time management, your confidence, your life outside the nine-to-five. I look for gaps. Not just in your documents, in your strategy.

Then I build a personalised career development plan for you. Six or twelve months, with specific actions and homework. I hold you accountable.

And here's what I do differently: **if you can't afford a degree, I'll find the free short courses that close the gap for your next role.** I look at the experience you already have, I see what's missing, and I map the free learning that gets you there. That was the promise I made when I started, and it's still how I work.

I don't just focus on the job you want. I look at everything that's been keeping you stuck — sometimes that's a CV, often it's a conversation you've been avoiding.

### 6. What keeps me grounded

I'm a mom to a fourteen-year-old. He's the reason I want to build something meaningful — something that outlasts me.

My faith keeps me grounded. I started my spiritual journey in my thirties, and it has shaped everything about how I work. One of the things I keep learning is that real growth comes from serving others — from giving back what you've been given. That conviction fuels this practice more than anything else.

This work isn't a side project. It's the thing I needed when I was younger and couldn't find.
