## TONE AND VOICE

This is a professional tool, not a cheerful freebie. The design should feel like something a SA corporate professional would keep on their desktop and refer to repeatedly — not a colourful social media template.

Think: clean, editorial, authoritative. The design of the *Financial Mail* or *Harvard Business Review* downloadables — structured, readable, clearly organised. Not pastel motivational quotes. Not big emojis. Not stock photo collages.

The copy throughout uses UK English (no American spellings).

### PAGE 3 — THE FORMULA

**Background:** Chai (#E4D8CB)
**Layout:** Two sections — formula at top, SA adjustments table below

**Heading (Noto Serif Display, Dark Gunmetal):** The Formula SA Recruiters Actually Reward

**Formula block (large, visually prominent):**

Display this as a styled block — not a table. Use clean boxes or a horizontal flow with pipe separators:

[ Target Role ] | [ Specialty or Domain ] | [ Proof or Impact ] | [ Credibility or Location ]

Use Noto Serif Display for the formula text. Each element in its own block with a Rodeo Dust separator between them. White background on the formula block itself.

**Subheading (Raleway bold, Dark Gunmetal):** SA-specific adjustments to global advice

**Table (Raleway, standard weight):**

Use a clean three-column table — no heavy borders, just subtle Latte lines:

| What global advice says | What SA reality requires | What to do instead |
|---|---|---|
| Use your current job title | SA recruiters search standard titles, not internal company designations. "Level III Associate" is invisible. | Use the most commonly searched title for your function, not your internal title |
| Add Open to Work | SA recruiters are split. Some see it as proactive; others use it to negotiate lower salaries. | Use "Open to [specific role] roles" in your headline rather than the generic banner |
| Include your city | SA recruiters search by city and province constantly | Always include your city. Add "Open to relocation" if relevant |
| Show quantified results | SA CVs describe duties, not achievements. Numbers make you stand out more here than in markets where it is standard. | Always quantify. Rough numbers beat vague claims. Use rands, percentages, team sizes |
| Be authentic | "Passionate leader" and "results-driven professional" are the most common SA headline mistakes | Replace adjectives with searchable keywords and quantified proof |
